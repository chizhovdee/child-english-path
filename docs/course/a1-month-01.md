# Month 1 — All About Me

**Stage:** Foundation (A1 → A2)

**Pace:** one block per day, ~20-30 minutes, 6 days per week, 1 rest day

## Week 1 — Hi, I'm...

### Block 1 — Core Phrases

Learn these 8 phrases. Read each one aloud 2-3 times.

- **Hi, I'm [name].** — "Hi, I'm Alex. What's your name?"
- **I'm [age] years old.** — "I'm 13 years old."
- **I'm from [city].** — "I'm from Moscow. It's a big city."
- **I'm a student.** — "I'm a student. I go to school every day."
- **Nice to meet you!** — "Hi, I'm Alex. Nice to meet you!"
- **I'm in 7th grade.** — "I'm in 7th grade. I like my class."
- **This is my first year at this school.** — "This is my first year at this school. Everything is new."
- **I'm new here.** — "I'm new here. Can you help me?"

### Block 2 — Example Text

Read this text. A teen writes an intro message in a gaming Discord server.

> Hey everyone! I'm Alex. I'm 13 years old and I'm from Moscow. I'm a student — I'm in 7th grade. This is my first year at this school, so I'm new here. I play Minecraft and Roblox every day after school. I also watch a lot of YouTube — mostly gaming videos. I want to practice my English with you guys. Nice to meet you all! Add me if you want to play together. See you in chat!

*~80 words*

### Block 3 — Speaking Practice

Read each prompt aloud. You can record yourself on your phone.

**Controlled** — say exactly this (use your real info):

- [ ] Say: "Hi, I'm [your name]."
- [ ] Say: "I'm [your age] years old."
- [ ] Say: "I'm from [your city]."
- [ ] Say: "I'm a student. I'm in [your grade]."
- [ ] Say: "Nice to meet you!"

**Guided** — answer these questions in a full sentence:

- [ ] What is your name?
- [ ] How old are you?
- [ ] Where are you from?
- [ ] What grade are you in?
- [ ] Are you new at your school, or have you been there for a long time?
- [ ] What is your city like? Is it big or small?

**Free** — speak freely:

- [ ] Introduce yourself to a new classmate — say 4-5 sentences.
- [ ] Tell about yourself for 30 seconds. Try not to stop!
- [ ] Imagine you join a new Discord server. Record a voice intro about yourself.
- [ ] You are at a summer camp. Introduce yourself to the group — name, age, city, and one fun fact about you.

### Block 5 — Writing Task

Include:

Read your text aloud when you finish.

### Block 6 — Fun Element: Celebrity Introduction

## Week 2 — My Day

### Block 1 — Core Phrases

Learn these 8 phrases. Read each one aloud 2-3 times.

- **I wake up at [time].** — "I wake up at 7 a.m. every day. It's early!"
- **I go to school.** — "I go to school at 8. I walk with my friend."
- **I have lunch at school.** — "I have lunch at school at 12. The food is okay."
- **I come home at [time].** — "I come home at 3 p.m. I'm always hungry!"
- **I do my homework.** — "I do my homework after school. It takes one hour."
- **I play games after school.** — "I play games after school. I love Minecraft."
- **I go to bed at [time].** — "I go to bed at 10 p.m. I'm tired."
- **I eat breakfast with my family.** — "I eat breakfast with my family every morning."

### Block 2 — Example Text

Read this text. A teen tells a friend about their day in a text message.

> Hey! So, my day is always the same. I wake up at 7 a.m. and eat breakfast with my family. Then I go to school at 8. I have lunch at school with my friends — we sit together every day. I come home at 3 p.m. First, I do my homework (boring!). After that, I play games — usually Roblox or Minecraft. In the evening, I watch YouTube. I go to bed at 10 p.m. That's my life! Pretty normal, right? 😄

*~80 words*

### Block 3 — Speaking Practice

Read each prompt aloud. You can record yourself on your phone.

**Controlled** — say exactly this (use your real info):

- [ ] Say: "I wake up at [your time]."
- [ ] Say: "I go to school at [your time]."
- [ ] Say: "I have lunch at [your time]."
- [ ] Say: "I come home at [your time]."
- [ ] Say: "I go to bed at [your time]."

**Guided** — answer these questions in a full sentence:

- [ ] What time do you wake up?
- [ ] What do you do after school?
- [ ] What do you do before bed?
- [ ] Do you eat breakfast? What do you eat?
- [ ] What is the first thing you do when you come home?
- [ ] What do you do on Saturday? Is it different from a school day?

**Free** — speak freely:

- [ ] Describe your morning — from waking up to leaving for school.
- [ ] Tell about your whole day from start to finish. Try to say 5-6 sentences!
- [ ] Describe your perfect day — what time do you wake up, what do you do?
- [ ] Compare your school day and your weekend. What is the same? What is different?

### Block 5 — Writing Task

- [ ] Write about your day (50-70 words). Start from morning, end with evening.

Use these time words:
- **first** — "First, I eat breakfast."
- **then** — "Then, I go to school."
- **after that** — "After that, I play games."
- **in the evening** — "In the evening, I watch YouTube."

Read your text aloud when you finish.

### Block 6 — Fun Element: Speed Game

Speed round! How many things you do in a day can you list in 60 seconds?

**Rules:**
1. Set a timer for 60 seconds.
2. Write sentences as fast as you can.
3. Each sentence must start with **"I..."**.
4. Count your sentences when the timer stops.

**Example:**
> I wake up. I brush my teeth. I eat breakfast. I go to school. I talk to friends...

Can you beat 10 sentences? Try it!

- [ ] My score: ___ sentences in 60 seconds.

## Week 3 — Things I Like

### Block 1 — Core Phrases

Learn these 8 phrases. Read each one aloud 2-3 times.

- **I like [noun].** — "I like pizza. I eat it every Friday!"
- **I love [noun].** — "I love music. I listen to it every day."
- **I don't like [noun].** — "I don't like homework. It's boring."
- **I hate [noun/activity].** — "I hate mornings. I want to sleep more!"
- **My favourite [thing] is [name].** — "My favourite game is Minecraft."
- **I like playing [game].** — "I like playing Roblox with my friends."
- **I really like [noun].** — "I really like YouTube. I watch it for hours."
- **I don't like [noun].** — "I don't like math. It's too hard for me."

### Block 2 — Example Text

Read this text. A teen is filling out a profile on a new gaming platform.

> Hey! My name is Alex and this is my profile. I like video games — my favourite game is Minecraft. I also love music. I really like hip-hop and pop. I like playing Roblox after school with my friends. I don't like math, but I like English — it's easy! I hate getting up early. My favourite food is pizza, and I don't like soup. That's me! Add me if you want to play!

*~80 words*

### Block 3 — Speaking Practice

Read each prompt aloud. You can record yourself on your phone.

**Controlled** — say exactly this (use your real info):

- [ ] Say: "I like [a food you like]."
- [ ] Say: "I love [a game you love]."
- [ ] Say: "I don't like [a thing you don't like]."
- [ ] Say: "My favourite [thing] is [name]."
- [ ] Say: "I hate [something you hate]."

**Guided** — answer these questions in a full sentence:

- [ ] What is your favourite food?
- [ ] What games do you play?
- [ ] What don't you like?
- [ ] What music do you listen to?
- [ ] What is your favourite school subject? Why?
- [ ] What is your favourite YouTube channel?
- [ ] Do you like sports? Which ones?

**Free** — speak freely:

- [ ] Tell about 5 things you like and 3 things you don't like.
- [ ] What is your perfect day? What do you do?
- [ ] You are making a "My Favourites" video for YouTube. Talk about your favourite food, game, music, and movie.
- [ ] Your friend asks: "What should I watch / play / eat?" Recommend something and explain why you like it.

## Week 4 — My People

### Block 1 — Core Phrases

Learn these 8 phrases. Read each one aloud 2-3 times.

- **I have a [family member].** — "I have a brother. He is 10 years old."
- **My best friend is [name].** — "My best friend is Dima. We play games together."
- **He/She is [adjective].** — "She is really funny. He is tall and kind."
- **She has [physical description].** — "She has brown hair and green eyes."
- **We go to the same school.** — "We go to the same school. Our class is cool."
- **His/Her name is [name].** — "Her name is Masha. His name is Kirill."
- **Her/His favourite [thing] is [name].** — "His favourite game is Brawl Stars. Her favourite food is sushi."
- **My family is [description].** — "My family is small — just me, my mum, and my dad."

### Block 2 — Example Text

Read this text. A teen writes a school assignment about their best friend and family.

> My best friend is Dima. He is 13, like me. He is tall and funny. He has dark hair and brown eyes. We go to the same school — we are in the same class! His favourite game is Brawl Stars, and we play it together every day after school. My family is not big. I have a mum, a dad, and a little brother. His name is Nikita. He is 9 years old. He is loud but kind. I love my family and my friends!

*~80 words*

### Block 3 — Speaking Practice

Read each prompt aloud. You can record yourself on your phone.

**Controlled** — say exactly this (use your real info):

- [ ] Say: "My best friend is [name]."
- [ ] Say: "He/She is [description — tall, funny, kind, smart...]."
- [ ] Say: "I have a [family member — brother, sister, mum, dad]."
- [ ] Say: "His/Her name is [name]."
- [ ] Say: "We go to the same school."

**Guided** — answer these questions in a full sentence:

- [ ] Who is your best friend? Tell about them.
- [ ] How many people are in your family?
- [ ] What do you and your friend do together?
- [ ] What does your best friend look like?
- [ ] What is your friend's favourite game or hobby?
- [ ] Do you have a brother or sister? What are they like?
- [ ] Who is the funniest person in your family? Why?

**Free** — speak freely:

- [ ] Describe your best friend — what they look like, their personality, what you do together. Try 5-6 sentences.
- [ ] Tell about your family — who is in it, what they are like.
- [ ] Imagine your friend is not here today. Tell a new classmate about them: "Let me tell you about my friend..."
- [ ] Describe someone in your family without saying their name. Can someone guess who it is?

### Block 6 — Fun Element: Roleplay

Imagine this: you meet your friend's new friend at school. You don't know each other yet!

**Step 1:** Introduce yourself. Use your phrases from **Week 1** — say your name, age, where you are from, and that you are a student.

**Step 2:** Now describe your best friend to this new person. Say what your friend looks like, what they like, and what you do together.

Try to say at least 5 sentences. You can write it down first, then say it aloud.

**Example:**

> Hi, I'm Alex! I'm 13 years old. I'm from Moscow. I'm a student — I'm in 7th grade. Nice to meet you! Let me tell you about my best friend. His name is Dima. He is tall and funny. He has dark hair. His favourite game is Brawl Stars. We play together every day!

- [ ] Try it! Introduce yourself and describe your best friend.

## Month 1 Checkpoint

You finished Month 1! Let's check what you can do.

### Speaking

Record yourself on your phone (1 minute):

"Tell about yourself. Include: your name, age, where you are from, your day, things you like, and your best friend."

Try to speak without stopping. It's okay to make mistakes!

### Writing

- [ ] Write about your best friend (60-80 words). Use phrases from all 4 weeks: introduce yourself, say what you do every day, what you like, and describe your friend.

### I can... (self-check)

Check everything you can do:

- [ ] I can introduce myself (name, age, city)
- [ ] I can talk about my daily routine
- [ ] I can say what I like and don't like
- [ ] I can describe my friend or family member
- [ ] I can write 50+ words about myself
- [ ] I can speak for 30+ seconds about myself

**If you checked 4 or more — great job! You are ready for Month 2.**

**If you checked less than 4 — go back and practise the weeks you found difficult. No rush!**
