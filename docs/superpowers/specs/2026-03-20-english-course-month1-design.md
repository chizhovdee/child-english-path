# English Course for Teen (13 y.o.) — Month 1 Design

## Summary

Self-study English course for a 13-year-old beginner (A1 → C1 over 24 months). This spec covers Month 1 only. Full course to be designed iteratively after validating the first month.

## Student Profile

- Age: 13
- Start level: A1 (beginner)
- Target: C1 (advanced)
- Format: self-study, no tutor, 20-30 min/day
- Language: all materials in English
- Interests: games, YouTube, school, friends, food — mixed evenly

## Course Parameters

- **Total duration:** 24 months
- **Structure:** monthly files, 4 weeks per month, 1 topic per week
- **Focus:** speaking + writing (communication-first)
- **No parallel tracks** (no exam prep, no travel track)
- **No listening/audio blocks** (text-based self-study format)

## Progression Plan (high-level, for context)

| Stage | Level | Months | Duration |
|-------|-------|--------|----------|
| Foundation | A1 → A2 | 1-6 | 6 months |
| Building | A2 → B1 | 7-12 | 6 months |
| Intermediate | B1 → B2 | 13-18 | 6 months |
| Advanced | B2 → C1 | 19-24 | 6 months |

## Month 1: "All About Me"

### Weekly Topics

| Week | Topic | Grammar (1 per week, implicit) |
|------|-------|-------------------------------|
| 1 | Hi, I'm... (name, age, where from) | to be (I am, you are) |
| 2 | My Day (routine, time, actions) | Present Simple (I wake up, I go) |
| 3 | Things I Like (hobbies, games, food) | like + noun ("I like pizza"), bonus: like + -ing |
| 4 | My People (family, friends, descriptions) | have/has, possessives (my, his, her) |

### Pacing

One block per day, 6 days per week, 1 rest day. Each block takes ~20-30 min. Student self-paces within the week.

### Deviations from CLAUDE.md

- **"Listening / Input"** → renamed to **"Example Text"** — the course is text-only self-study, no audio. The example text serves the same purpose (comprehensible input).
- **"Mini Explanation"** → folded into **"Example Text"** — grammar is shown through examples in the text, not as a separate block. No grammar lectures per CLAUDE.md philosophy.
- **"Practice" section** → split into **Speaking Practice** + **Practice Exercises** + **Writing Task** for better self-study scaffolding.

### Target: ~25 active phrases by end of Month 1

### Week Structure (7 blocks per week)

Each week follows this exact structure:

#### 1. Core Phrases (5-10)
Phrases in context, not isolated words. Show each phrase in a natural sentence.

Example for Week 1:
- "Hi, I'm Alex" (not: hi = привет)
- "I'm 13 years old"
- "I'm from Moscow"

#### 2. Example Text (60-100 words)
Short text or mini-dialogue using the week's phrases. Teen context (school, online, friends).

#### 3. Speaking Practice (7-10 prompts)
Student reads prompts aloud and optionally records on phone. No partner needed. Free practice prompts can be done as self-talk.

Progression from controlled to free:
- Controlled (3-4): repeat/substitute phrases ("Say: Hi, I'm [your name]")
- Guided (3-4): answer questions using phrases ("How old are you?")
- Free (1-2): open tasks ("Tell about yourself")

#### 4. Practice Exercises (3-5 items)
Scaffolded written exercises to internalize patterns before free production:
- Fill the gap: "I ___ 13 years old." (am / is / are)
- Match: match phrases to pictures or situations
- Reorder: put words in the correct order ("am / I / Alex / Hi")

#### 5. Writing Task (50-80 words at A1)
Simple structured writing using the week's phrases and grammar.

#### 6. Fun Element (1 per week)
Rotating types: quiz, challenge, roleplay, game. Must be doable solo without a tutor.

Examples:
- Quiz: "True or false about the example text"
- Challenge: "Introduce yourself as a famous person"
- Game: "How many sentences about yourself in 60 seconds?"
- Roleplay: "You meet a new student at school. What do you say?"

#### 7. Key Phrases to Remember (5-7)
End-of-week summary. The phrases the student should know by heart.

### Month Checkpoint

At the end of Month 1:
- **Speaking:** Record yourself: "Tell about yourself for 1 minute" (phone recording)
- **Writing:** Write about your best friend (60-80 words)
- **Self-check:** Checklist of "I can..." statements

### Content Guidelines

- All text in simple English (A1 graded)
- Max 1 new grammar concept per week, taught through examples (no grammar lectures)
- Contexts: school, friends, YouTube, games, food — rotated across weeks
- Phrases > words. Always show usage in sentences.
- Fun element is mandatory, not optional
- No complex instructions — the 13-year-old should understand what to do without help

## Quality Criteria

Each week's content must be:
- Clear and self-explanatory (no tutor needed)
- Age-appropriate (13 y.o. teenager)
- Not boring (variety, fun elements, relatable topics)
- Practically useful (real communication, not textbook exercises)
- Following phrase-based approach (no word lists)
